Course Syllabus

An overview of the course, objectives, schedule, and grading criteria.

Course Overview

This course explores the evolving role of artists in today’s creative economy, examining key shifts in labor, policy, and identity that define creative professionals as "human capital." Through interactive modules, students will engage with the historical and economic contexts that shape the creative workforce today.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the historical shift in the artist’s role within the creative economy.
  • Analyze the concept of "human capital" as it applies to creative professionals.
  • Examine the impact of market-oriented skills in art education.
  • Evaluate the socio-economic effects of the "creative economy" on artists and other creative workers.

Grading Criteria

Your final grade will be based on the following components:

  • Quizzes: 20% - Short quizzes at the end of each module to assess understanding.
  • Assignments: 30% - Application-based assignments exploring module concepts.
  • Final Project: 40% - A comprehensive analysis or creative project on a course topic.
  • Participation: 10% - Active engagement in discussions and course activities.

Course Policies

We are committed to creating a respectful and inclusive learning environment. Please review the following policies:

  • Attendance: Regular attendance is expected to keep up with discussions and activities.
  • Late Work: Late submissions will incur a penalty unless prior arrangements are made.
  • Academic Integrity: Students are expected to uphold the highest standards of integrity and honesty in their work.
