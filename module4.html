Module 4: Building an Inclusive Online Learning Community

  1. EDTECH 201
  2. Content
  3. Module 4
Learning Objectives
  • Enumerate the interpersonal and social aspects of teaching and learning.
  • Recognize the role of instructor presence, peer interaction, and creative activities in building an engaging community.
  • Apply gamified elements (e.g., badges, points) and creative thinking techniques to design inclusive community-building activities.
  • Moderate discussions and provide constructive feedback that encourages creativity and respects diverse perspectives.
Content

What the drive for creativity has produced is not just an abnormal notion of working life, a replacement of expertise, skills and qualifications with the idea of a portfolio into which fits a whole range of wildly different capacities, but also a highly individualized outlook.

Angela McRobbie in Be Creative: Making a Living in the New Culture Industries (2016)

The quote above describes the shift in today’s creative industry toward individual portfolios and self-branding over shared expertise. In this module, we’ll learn how fostering creative collaboration can build an inclusive community that values collective growth and mutual support.

Teaching and learning are deeply social activities that rely on interpersonal relationships and social interactions (Wilson and Opperwall, n.d.). These aspects are crucial for creating a supportive and effective learning environment:

  • Transactional Distance: refers to the psychological and communication space between instructors and learners. High transactional distance can lead to feelings of isolation and disengagement. Reducing this distance through active communication and interaction is essential for fostering a sense of community.
  • Community of Inquiry Framework: emphasizes three types of presence necessary for a successful online learning community: cognitive presence (engagement with the content), teaching presence (instructor’s role in guiding and facilitating learning), and social presence (interpersonal relationships and interactions among students).
  • Social Connection and Community: helps students feel connected and supported. This can be achieved through regular interactions, collaborative activities, and creating opportunities for students to engage with each other and the instructor.

Encouraging productive interactions in an online learning environment is essential for several reasons (University of Waterloo, n.d.):

Enhanced Learning Experience

Productive interactions between peers and between students and instructors enrich the learning experience. They provide opportunities for students to discuss ideas, clarify doubts, and deepen their understanding of the subject matter.

Sense of Community

Regular and meaningful interactions help build a sense of community among students. This sense of belonging can reduce feelings of isolation and increase student engagement and motivation.

Development of Soft Skills

Engaging in discussions and collaborative activities helps students develop essential soft skills such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving.

Diverse Perspectives

Interacting with peers from different backgrounds, viewpoints, and skills exposes students to a variety of perspectives, enhancing their critical thinking and broadening their understanding.


By fostering these interactions, educators can create a more dynamic, inclusive, and effective online learning environment.

Designing creative and inclusive activities involves considering the diverse needs and interests of students (Wilson and Opperwall, n.d.; A. Després-Bedward et al., 2018):

  • Icebreakers and Small-Group Discussions
    • Use icebreakers and small-group discussions to help students get to know each other and share their backgrounds and experiences.
    • Example: Setting up breakout rooms in video conferencing programs such as Zoom or Microsoft Teams.
  • Gamified Activities
    • See Module 2: Principles of Gamification for Learning.
  • Creative Projects
    • Design projects that encourage creative expression and collaboration.
    • Example: Conducting virtual scavenger hunts, storytelling sessions, and collaborative projects can engage students and build community.
  • Inclusive Facilitation
    • Facilitate activities in a way that ensures all voices are heard and respected.
    • Example: Using open-ended questions and providing multiple ways for students to participate, such as writing, speaking, or even creating memes.

  1. Constructive Feedback
    • Purpose: Provide specific, growth-oriented feedback that encourages improvement in a supportive way.
    • Examples:
      • “I appreciate your insight on [topic]. Could you expand on how this connects to [related concept]?”
      • “This point is strong! You might consider adding [specific detail] to make it even clearer.”
      • “Great analysis! How could you further support this argument with examples from [resource]?”
  2. Moderation for Inclusivity
    • Purpose: Maintain respectful discussions by setting communication guidelines, modelling respectful disagreement, and intervening as needed.
    • Examples:
      • “Thanks for sharing your viewpoint. Can we consider other perspectives, like [alternative view]?”
      • “It seems we have differing opinions here—let’s explore each side respectfully. What evidence supports your stance?”

    • Examples:

      • “Playing ‘devil’s advocate,’ what do you think might be a potential challenge to this idea?”
      • “Does anyone have a personal experience that relates to this topic? How did it shape your view?”

In this Course

In this course, our inclusive community-building interventions include:

Reddit Community with Weekly AMA Sessions

A dedicated Reddit community fosters open discussion and connection among learners, with weekly “Ask Me Anything” sessions to encourage engagement and real-time interaction with instructors.

Kudoboard for Peer Recognition

An interactive Kudoboard lets students give shoutouts to their peers, celebrating achievements and creating a supportive, positive environment that values each participant’s contributions.

Shared Spotify Playlist for Study Tracks

A collaborative Spotify playlist allows learners to share favorite study tracks, adding a personal touch that enhances connection and builds a shared community culture.
